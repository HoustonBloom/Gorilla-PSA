# Fix Houston Traffic — Gorilla PSA

A grassroots traffic PSA initiative for Houston. No brand. No budget. No organization. Just citizens doing the public education the city won't.

## What is this?

Houston drivers lose nearly 4 full days a year sitting in traffic. Research shows the zipper merge — a simple driving technique — can cut backups by 40–50%. But most Houstonians have never heard of it because no one has told them.

A **Gorilla PSA** is citizens filling that gap: calling radio stations, telling friends, sharing the data, and repeating it until the behavior changes.

## The site

Single-page HTML site that serves as the initiative's hub:

- **The problem** — Houston traffic data, worst corridors, why adding lanes doesn't work
- **The solution** — Side-by-side animation showing zipper merge vs early merge
- **Gorilla PSA explainer** — What it is and why citizens have to do it
- **The script** — A 30-second talking point anyone can deliver
- **Radio station guide** — Houston call-in shows with phone numbers
- **Evidence** — Sourced research from state DOTs, universities, and AAA
- **Participation tracker** — Log actions to see collective momentum

## Stack

Single `index.html` file. No frameworks, no build step, no dependencies beyond Google Fonts. CSS-only animations. Vanilla JS for interactivity.

## Based on

- MnDOT, MoDOT, CDOT, FHWA zipper merge studies
- Texas A&M Transportation Institute research
- SEH Inc. microsimulation data
- Michigan ITRE/NC State field study
- Houston TranStar / TomTom / TTI congestion data
